Introduction to the Internet

What is the Internet?

The Internet is a global network of interconnected computers that allows users to share information and communicate with each other. It works by transmitting data through a system of servers and routers using a variety of protocols, including HTTP, TCP/IP, and DNS. The Internet enables access to vast amounts of information, websites, and services, such as email, social media, and online shopping. It is decentralized, meaning no single entity controls it, and it continues to evolve as new technologies and uses emerge.
